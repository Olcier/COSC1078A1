Project Ideas

  • Overview

My goal here is to build a product that will enable visionary sensors of people who are unable to see. As we know, sight is the ability for eyes to take in data gained through the reflection of light on objects. This stimulates the the brain creating image. In easier terms, visual data is only data that stimulates our visual organs which in turns creates an image in our brains. What I plan to achieve, is to take in that data through artificial methods and providing the same stimuli to the brain tricking the brain into thinking that the data has been provided through the eyes and thus will create an image for people who are visually impaired.

  • Motivation

This world is a beautiful place but there are many who do not possess the gift of sight. For those who were born unfortunate, or had lost their vision later on through accidents or any other events, I want to find a remedy for such situations. Of course, the artificial method would not be as polished as our own natural eyes, neither can we think to do so, but when has that stopped anyone from moving forward? Knowing our limitations, it would still be a success if one is able see because my work. This isn't a project one can do alone, but with a good enough team, it isn't entirely impossible either. In my own terms, my motivation are my own people whose life may change for the better through my inventions.

  • Description

My main objective is to help the visually impaired, therefore the aim of our product is to serve the visonless. Since I'm at the primary stage of my project, when it comes to polish, much will be left desired. However, my idea to send visual signals is through Electrical brain stimulation. From previous research and studies, it has been concluded that nerves and muscles are electrically exciteable. Since parts of the brain has been stimulated through this process, my aim for now is to improve upon this concept and to make it much more precise. Hence, by collecting the data required, it could be possible to send visual stimuli to the brain in forms of electricity, exciting the optical lobe by certain amounts. Since this experiment was done to stimulate the motor cortex, I am hopeful the same could be done to the optical lobe, the part of the brain which is responsible for our vision.

In order to achieve this milestone, I need a group of IT specialists including myself to handle the data. Inorder to implement the idea, the physical representation of the project, we will need to consult eachother to find out which way would be the safest way to execute our idea, as there could be a lot of risks involved for the user if not handled properly. Hence, we will need to settle for a prototype, which is not only the safest option but also within the price range of the average user so it doesn't become out of reach of the people. Considering the general user would be the visually impaired, we would have to make sure that it doesn't become over-complicated to use either.

Lastly, I would reckon we would need a group of neuro-biologists to help us in our project. Their feedback to the project would be essential. Together, it is possible to meet the needed requirements to come up with a plusible solution. The aid could be fitted near the back of the head because that is the nearest position to the optical lobe. Our job is to ensure the electrical flow is regulated properly, and there are no system failures. The consequences could be drastic, so keeping failure in mind we need to design the prototype in a manner that it can shut down on its own before such scenerios. We have to take our users safety as the utmost priority, multiple fail safes is due. Before releasing the product, it has to pass different test phases such as different weather conditions. In case of humid climate, we have to make sure the sweat does not affect the product of our project. So, we need to design the product keeping differnt scenerios in mind.
